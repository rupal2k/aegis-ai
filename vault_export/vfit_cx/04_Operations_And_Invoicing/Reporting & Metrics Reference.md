# Reporting & Metrics Reference

Quick reference for what CX can request, how to frame it, and what to clarify before sharing it with a client.

---

## Common Report Types

| Report type | Typical use | Typical owner |
|---|---|---|
| Active-user count | Billing and dispute review | Data team |
| Challenge participation summary | Post-challenge recap | Data team or admin export |
| Engagement trend summary | Client health checks and reviews | Data team |
| Department breakdown | Leadership-facing visibility | Data team |
| Wellness trend summary | Broader program review | Data team |
| ROI-oriented summary | Executive check-ins and renewals | Data team plus CS review |

---

## Common Metrics To Clarify Up Front

- invited users
- registered users
- active users
- challenge participants
- completion rate
- steps or distance totals
- trend by week or month
- department-level breakdown

Do not assume two teams mean the same thing by "engagement." Define it before the request goes out.

---

## Reporting Rules

- Always confirm the exact date range
- Always confirm the metric definition
- Exclude test accounts if the report is commercial or client-facing
- Label the program or challenge name clearly when presenting results
- Prefer aggregated reporting over individual sensitive health detail

For health-related views, the safest client-facing default is: **HR sees trends, not private individual health data.**

---

## Request Checklist

Before you ask for a report, confirm:

- client name as it appears in the platform
- date range
- exact metric needed
- purpose of the request
- deadline
- whether the output is internal-only or client-facing

---

## Suggested Lead Times

| Request | Lead time |
|---|---|
| Standard billing count | 2 to 3 business days |
| Standard challenge summary | 3 to 5 business days |
| More complex reporting or custom cuts | 5 or more business days |

If the request is tied to invoice timing or a renewal meeting, say that explicitly.

---

## Good Questions To Ask The Client

- What business question are you trying to answer?
- Do you want a summary or raw supporting detail?
- Is this for billing, renewal, leadership review, or employee comms?
- Do you need a one-time report or a repeating cadence?

Those questions prevent unnecessary rework and help the data team deliver the right shape the first time.
