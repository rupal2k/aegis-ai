# Product & Backend Support

Guide for escalating platform issues, feature clarifications, and bug reports to product and engineering teams.

---

## When To Escalate To Product Or Backend

| Situation | Action |
|---|---|
| Platform feature does not work as expected | Bug report |
| Client reports data is not syncing | Backend or integration escalation |
| Feature request from client | Product feedback |
| Platform behavior conflicts with agreed documentation | Clarification request |
| Critical issue affecting a live challenge | Priority escalation |

---

## Common Product Areas To Mention Clearly

When writing an escalation, name the product area as precisely as possible:

- user management and roster access
- challenge engine or leaderboard behavior
- rewards or redemption behavior
- reports and analytics
- notifications
- connected devices or sync flow
- mindfulness, nutrition, or other activity logging flows

The more specific the area, the faster the issue gets routed.

---

## Bug Reporting Process

### Step 1 - Confirm It Looks Like A Platform Issue

Before escalating:

- [ ] Reproduce the issue or confirm it is reproducible
- [ ] Check whether this is a client setup error
- [ ] Check whether the issue is isolated or broader
- [ ] Check for any known incident or maintenance notice

### Step 2 - Capture The Right Details

Always try to collect:

- affected company and user(s)
- exact steps to reproduce
- expected vs actual behavior
- screenshots or screen recordings
- exact error text
- device type, browser, app version, and timezone if relevant
- whether the issue involves Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin, or phone-only tracking

### Step 3 - Submit The Escalation

- Use the agreed internal channel or ticket flow
- Include severity
- Include business impact
- Include whether a live challenge or billing outcome is affected

### Step 4 - Follow Through

- log the ticket ID in CRM or account notes
- set a follow-up reminder
- keep the client updated until resolution or workaround

## Severity Guide

| Severity | Definition | Response target |
|---|---|---|
| Critical | Platform down, active challenge broken, serious data-impacting issue | Same day |
| High | Core feature broken for multiple users | Within 24 hours |
| Medium | Important issue with a workaround | Within 3 business days |
| Low | Cosmetic or minor inconvenience | Next planned cycle |

---

## Feature Request Rules

When a client asks for a new feature:

1. thank them and capture the use case
2. do not promise a delivery timeline
3. document business value and urgency
4. pass it to product through the agreed channel
5. tell the client it has been shared as feedback
