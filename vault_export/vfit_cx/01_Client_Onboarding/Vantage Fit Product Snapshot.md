# Vantage Fit Product Snapshot

Internal quick reference for CX onboarding, discovery, and client education.

---

## One-Line Description

Vantage Fit is a corporate wellness platform employees love to use. It combines native mobile apps, a web dashboard, an admin portal, gamified challenges, and real rewards.

---

## Core Product Pillars

| Pillar | What it includes | Why clients care |
|---|---|---|
| Activity tracking | Steps, active minutes, manual activity logging, wearable sync | Easy daily participation and measurable movement |
| Challenges and gamification | Race, streak, journey, level-up, marathon, multi-activity, training, templates | Drives adoption through structure, competition, and milestones |
| Health and wellness | Wellness score, HRA, vitals/goals, mindfulness, lab-report insights | Supports physical, mental, and cardiac wellness in one place |
| Nutrition | Food logging, calorie tracking | Expands the program beyond step counting |
| Community | Teams, groups, chats, wellness events | Helps wellness become social, not solitary |
| Rewards | Points, badges, gift cards, marketplace redemption | Gives employees real motivation to stay engaged |
| Admin platform | Dashboard, reports, notifications, user management, surveys | Gives HR visibility and control |
| Integrations | Apple Watch, Fitbit, Garmin, Vantage Circle ecosystem | Fits into what employees and admins already use |

---

## Top Differentiators

1. Native mobile experience that supports real adoption
2. Gamification plus real rewards, not badges alone
3. Full-spectrum wellness across physical, mental, and nutritional habits
4. Admin analytics that help HR show program impact
5. Works well alongside the broader Vantage Circle ecosystem

---

## What Client Admins Usually Need To Understand Early

- How employees will join: CSV upload, self-registration, or HRIS-supported flow
- Which challenge style is best for the first 30 days
- Whether rewards, points, or leaderboard visibility are part of the rollout
- Who needs admin access and reporting visibility
- How device sync should be explained to employees
- What success will be measured by: registrations, active users, participation, or engagement

---

## Good First-Launch Pattern

For a new client, the simplest strong launch usually looks like:

1. Admin access and employee roster setup
2. One short, low-friction challenge
3. Clear comms around rewards and how to participate
4. One early adoption check in the first 48 hours
5. One summary report after the first challenge closes

This keeps the first experience manageable while giving the client something tangible to react to.

---

## Important CX Framing

- Speak to the HR buyer first: adoption, engagement, visibility, and ROI
- Speak to the employee benefit second: easy, fun, rewarding, and habit-building
- For health data, emphasize aggregated trends for HR rather than individual sensitive detail
