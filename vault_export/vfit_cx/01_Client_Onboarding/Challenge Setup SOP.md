# Challenge Setup SOP

Standard operating procedure for configuring challenges on Vantage Fit.

---

## Overview

Every challenge setup follows the same 8-step flow. Complete each step in order and do not skip QC or approval.

---

## Step 1 - Understand the Challenge Goal

Before touching the platform, confirm with the client:

- What is the primary outcome: engagement, movement, habit-building, or team bonding?
- Is there a season, event, or campaign driving this launch?
- What is the audience size?
- Are rewards involved?
- Does the client want a simple first launch or a more advanced program?

---

## Step 2 - Confirm Duration and Audience

| Parameter | Details to confirm |
|---|---|
| Start date | Exact date, with setup lead time |
| End date | Exact end date or total duration |
| Participants | All employees or a defined group |
| Team setup | Whether the challenge is individual, team-based, or company-wide |

**Recommended setup lead time:** 3 to 5 business days before launch.

---

## Step 3 - Select the Right Challenge Type

| Challenge type | Best for | Typical examples |
|---|---|---|
| Race / competition | Straightforward leaderboard energy | Steps, distance, calories |
| Streak | Daily habit formation | Hydration, mindfulness, reading, daily activity |
| Journey / route-based | Storytelling and milestone progress | Virtual walks, destination challenges |
| Level-up | Progressive goals over time | Increasing activity targets |
| E-marathon | Endurance and distance focus | Running or walking distance campaigns |
| Multi-activity | Broad wellness participation | Steps plus hydration plus mindfulness |
| Training | Structured programs | Workout plans, coaching-style progression |
| Templated | Fastest launch path | Pre-built challenges for onboarding or seasonal use |

**Important:** Hydration, mindfulness, reading, or nutrition are often themes or tracked activities inside a streak or multi-activity setup rather than a separate underlying challenge engine.

---

## Step 4 - Define Milestones and Rewards

- Set milestone checkpoints
- Confirm reward type: points, badges, gift cards, custom prizes
- Define any leaderboard prizes
- Record reward commitments clearly for later billing or fulfillment review

---

## Step 5 - Configure Challenge

1. Navigate to **Challenges -> Create New Challenge**
2. Enter challenge name, description, and banner image
3. Set challenge type, goals, and tracked metric(s)
4. Set dates and audience
5. Configure milestones and rewards
6. Add rules or bonus mechanics if needed
7. Save and preview

---

## Step 6 - Internal QC Check

Before sharing with the client, verify:

- [ ] Challenge name and description are correct
- [ ] Dates are accurate
- [ ] Participant group is correct
- [ ] Milestones and reward values are correct
- [ ] Banner image displays properly
- [ ] Challenge type fits the stated goal
- [ ] No typos or confusing instructions appear in visible text

---

## Step 7 - Client Approval

- Send a preview screenshot or link
- Confirm all details are correct
- Get explicit written approval before launch
- Log the approval in CRM or notes

---

## Step 8 - Launch and Monitor

- Launch on the confirmed start date
- Confirm the client has employee communication ready
- Monitor participation in the first 48 hours
- Flag low adoption early with practical suggestions
- Check in at midpoint and close

---

## Post-Challenge

- Export participation and results data
- Share a summary with the client
- Capture lessons for the next challenge
- Use the outcome to set up the next engagement or renewal conversation
